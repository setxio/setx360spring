## 2. The 3-Tier Separation of Powers

To prevent local political gridlock while ensuring absolute financial transparency, the Antigravity smart contracts (`SETXCouncilManager.sol`) divide network governance into three immutable tiers:

### Tier 1: Automated Protocols (Code is Law)
Basic transactional functions require **zero human approval** and cannot be paused or intercepted by the Digital Council:
- Processing retail marketplace orders.
- Elastically minting `SETX` stablecoins upon a Stripe fiat deposit.
- Routing automated 99% payouts to local merchants and streaming payroll to gig drivers.

### Tier 2: Council Decisions (The Digital Council Multi-Sig)
The council manages the deployment of unencumbered treasury interest generated by the DUNA's reserves (e.g., Ondo Treasury yield). Major operational actions require a strict **3-of-5 Consensus Threshold**:
- Approving a $15,000 disaster relief grant to local clean-up crews after a tropical storm.
- Contracting a third-party developer to build a regional utility widget.
- Adjusting base network gas reserve thresholds.

### Tier 3: Sovereign Veto (The General Public)
If the Digital Council goes rogue, attempts to misallocate funds, or acts against the community's interest, the general public holds the ultimate fallback lever. Because every verified citizen holds an immutable `SETXIdentity` Soulbound Token, they can trigger an on-chain referendum. If **1,000 citizens** cast a veto ballot, the protocol automatically cancels the pending council action or revokes a representative's cryptographic keypair.

## 3. Term Limits & Staggered Elections Architecture

To prevent regional "digital career politicians" from entrenching themselves or gatekeeping the Multi-Sig keys, the smart contracts enforce strict programmatic term limits:

| Parameter | Duration / Limit | Operational Reasoning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Term Length** | 2 Years (730 Days) | 1 Year is too short to master the municipal codebase; 3+ Years risks technological stagnation. |
| **Consecutive Cap** | 2 Terms (4 Years Max) | Enforces fresh civic leadership and prevents keypair monopolization. |
| **Cool-Down Period** | 2 Years (730 Days) | After serving 4 consecutive years, a representative is programmatically locked out of the candidate pool for 1 full term. |

### Staggered Election Cycles (The Continuity Safeguard)
To preserve institutional memory and prevent a total wipeout of the council's operational momentum, elections are staggered across alternating years:
- **Year A**: Elect representatives for Groves/Nederland (Seat 4) and Orange (Seat 3).
- **Year B**: Elect representatives for Beaumont (Seat 1) and Port Arthur (Seat 2).
